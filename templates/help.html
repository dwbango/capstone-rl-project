Help & Documentation

Welcome to the Blackjack Simulation tool. This page provides an overview of the available parameters, advanced statistical features, and how to interpret the detailed records generated by the simulations.

Parameters

  • RL Method: Choose one of:
    • QLearning (Off-Policy) – Learns an optimal strategy from both exploratory and exploitative actions concurrently.
    • Sarsa (On-Policy) – Updates its strategy based on the actual sequence of states/actions/rewards taken while following its current policy.
    • BasicStrategy – A fixed, non-learning approach derived from widely recognized blackjack heuristics.
    • Random – A simple baseline agent that chooses actions randomly.
  • Number of Decks (1-8): Number of decks used in the shoe. More decks generally reduce the swings in card counts.
  • Max Splits (1-3): Maximum number of times a pair can be split, influencing complexity and EV in advanced play.
  • Shoes to Play: The total number of shoes to simulate in a single run.
  • Shuffle Point (Deck Penetration): Where in the shoe the cards are reshuffled, expressed as a fraction (e.g. 0.25 means reshuffle after 75% of the shoe is dealt).
  • Betting Style: Either flat (same wager every hand) or spread (varies bet based on count or other logic).

Actions

  • Run Simulation: Execute a bulk simulation of blackjack hands based on your chosen parameters.
  • Compare All 4 Methods: Runs multiple strategies (Random, BasicStrategy, QLearning, Sarsa) in one go, producing comparative results and multi-line graphs.
  • Run Stats (ANOVA): Performs repeated runs for each method, then applies ANOVA/post-hoc tests to assess whether EV differs significantly among strategies. Includes confidence intervals (CI) for EV.
  • Generate Report: Downloads a CSV with summary metrics, expanded hand-level data (outcomes, actions, decisions), and shoe-level card orders.
  • Generate Strategy Chart (RL): Heatmap-like chart for QLearning/Sarsa to show decision preferences (hit, stand, etc.) under different totals/dealer upcards.
  • Show Epsilon Chart (RL): Graphically displays how the exploration rate (epsilon) changes over time in QLearning/Sarsa.
  • Toggle Analytics Summary: Shows/hides an on-screen JSON summary of results (EV, final bankroll, etc.) for quick reference.

Interpreting Results

Key Metrics Include:

  • win_rate / loss_rate / push_rate: Distribution of outcomes across the simulation.
  • EV_per_hand: Average profit or loss per hand, essential for evaluating long-term expectations.
  • Final Bankroll: Overall performance relative to the initial bankroll.
  • Variance: Indicates how volatile the results can be.
  • Confidence Intervals (CI): Estimated range for metrics like EV, giving you a sense of the statistical precision.
  • ANOVA & Post-hoc Tests: Evaluate whether multiple strategies differ significantly in EV. The p-value and alpha level (commonly 0.05) guide whether differences are statistically significant.

When analyzing results, you can cross-reference win/loss/push rates, track EV over time, or compare final bankrolls. The Random baseline helps illustrate how a learning agent or a fixed BasicStrategy compares to purely random actions.

Use the expanded hand-level records to investigate specific outcomes, actions taken (splits, double downs, etc.), and how bet spreads or shuffle points influence results. The CSV also includes the order of cards in each shoe for deeper analysis.

For more details or troubleshooting, consult the project documentation or contact the support team.
